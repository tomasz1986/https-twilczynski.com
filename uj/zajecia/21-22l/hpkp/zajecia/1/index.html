Historia polityczna Korei Południowej po 1988 roku

Najważnejsze wydarzenia w historii politycznej Korei w latach 1945-1953

Literatura

The Association of Korean Teachers. 2010. Korean History for International Readers (pp. 270-289).pdf

Conflicts During the Cold War Period

  • rise of the United States and the Soviet Union as two super powers of the global after World War II
  • division of the world into the realm of capitalism and the realm of communism, and collision between the two
  • Europe divided into East and West, and Vietnam and Korea into North and South
  • significant shift of the dynamics of East Asia
    • civil war between the nationalists and communists in China
    • withdrawal of Kuomingtang to Taiwan, and establishment of the People's Republic of China on the mainland
    • Japan with a pivotal role in the anti-communist efforts led by the United States
  • Korean Peninsula as the front line in the conflict between Communists and Capitalists

1. Upon Liberation, the Process for Building a Nation Accelerates

Preparations for founding a nation

  • unconditional surrender of Japan to the Allied Forces on August 15, 1945
  • independence of Korea promised in the Cairo Declaration of 1943
  • passionate preparations of liberated Koreans to reconstruct their state
  • launch of the Preparation Committee for the National Foundation with branches throughout the country
    • aspirations to find an independent and democratic country
    • declaration of the foundation of the Joseon People's Republic on September 6, 1945

The US and USSR forces occupied Korea and divided it into two

  • Korean and Chinese nationalist movement as the main force against Japan before 1941
  • joining the Allied Forced by the United States in 1941, and by the Soviet Union in 1945
  • Koreans' wishes to establish their own government ignored by the United States and the Soviet Union
  • occupation of the South by Americans, and the North by the Soviets under the pretext of disarming the Japanese troops
  • establishment of military governments and disregard for the Provisional Government of Korea
  • support for the People's Committees organised from below by the USSR
  • employment of policemen and officials working for the Japanese by the US

Confrontations between the Left and Right: hindrance to founding a single government

  • return of many patriotic leaders after the liberation of Korea
    • return of Syngman Rhee from the United States and Kim Gu from China to Seoul
    • return of Kim Il Sung from the Soviet Union and Kim Tu Bong from China to Pyongyang
  • formation of numerous political parties and association all over the Korean Peninsula
  • divisions between the leaders lack of experience of working together in the same organisation
  • Moscow Conference of Foreign Ministers from the US, UK, and USSR in December 1945
    • discussing the procedures for Korean independence
    • formation of a joint committee to launch a provisional Korean government
    • trusteeship administered by the US, UK, USSR, and China
  • trusteeship criticised by the nationalists, and welcomed by the socialists
  • deepening confrontation between the Left and Right

2. Establishment of the Republic of Korea

Beginning of the division

  • withdrawal of all Japanese troops from Korea after the end of the war
  • economic crisis due to the Japanese-reliant industry and technology
  • most heavy industry located in the north, and most light industry located in the south
  • foundation of a new government and extensive reforms required to achieve social stability
  • United States and Soviet Union as the obstacles to establishing a united Korean government
  • stabilisation in the North
    • land reforms to liquidate pro-Japanese properties and nationalise all industry
    • laws protecting the rights of labourers and the equality of men and women
    • right-wing supporters fleeing to the South
  • the South pressured by the changes in the North
    • generosity and lack of punishment for pro-Japanese figures
    • unification under the name of anti-Communism to form a separate government
    • intensification of anti-US resistance of impoverished farmers and labourers
  • failed efforts to cooperate between the Left and Right, and between the US and USSR

Establishing the Government of the Republic of Korea

  • intensification of the Cold War and American-Soviet confrontation around the world since 1947
  • the Korean issue brought to the United Nations by the US in late-1947
    • nationwide elections to be held throughout the country under the UN supervision
    • Soviet refusal to endorse the decision until foreign troops are withdrawn from Korea
    • UN's decision to hold the elections in South Korea only
  • meeting of South and North Korean leaders in Pyongyang in 1948
  • brutal suppression of fierce protests on Jeju against the South-only elections
  • election of 198 members of the Assembly in South Korea on May 10, 1948
  • Syngman Rhee elected as the first President of the Republic of Korea on July 20, 1948
  • formation of the government, enaction of the constitution, and proclamation of the Republic of Korea on August 15, 1948
  • proclamation of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea in the North on September 9, 1948

A division after 1,300 years of unification

  • Republic of Korea as a successor of the Provisional Government of the Republic of Korea
  • foundation of the ROK at the cost of the peninsula's unity
  • division of the Korean Peninsula after having remained unified for 1,300 years
  • the tentative 38th parallel as the dividing line between the South and North
  • lack of power among Koreans to oppose the decision of the United States and Soviet Union

3. Outbreak of the Korean War

The anti-Communist system established by the Yi Seung-man (Syngman Rhee) administration

  • enaction of the Land Reform Law and the Punishment Law on Anti-National Actions
  • resistance against the laws by landlords and pro-Japanese Koreans supporting the new regime
  • Syngman Rhee's decision to protect his supporters under the justification of anti-communism
  • discontinuation of punishments of pro-Japanese and anti-national perpetrators
  • lack of basic civil rights for those labelled communist (bbalgaengi)
  • incomplete land reform failing to improve the farmers' living conditions

South and North plunged into a civil war

  • preparations for war by the North Korean government since 1949
  • official meetings of the North Korean leaders with Mao Zedong and Joseph Stalin in early-1950
  • hostility between the North and South escalated in the spring of 1950
  • spread of propaganda fliers and frequent clashes near the 38th parallel

Outbreak of war

  • attack of North Korean forces forward the 38th parallel on June 25, 1950
  • capture of Seoul, the capital of South Korea on June 28, 1950
  • almost complete unification of the Korean Peninsula by September 1950
  • United States-led UN forces joining the war since September 1950
  • successful Incheon Landing Operation on September 15, 1950 changing the tides of war
  • retreat of the North Korean army, and push of the allied forces of the UN and ROK into the North
  • occupation of most of the North Korean territory by October 1950
  • dispatch of large numbers of Chinese troops to help the North Korean regime
  • the second fall of Seoul in early 1951 to the North Korean and Chinese forces
  • push of the allied UN forces little above the 38th parallel
  • proposition of a ceasefire by the Soviet Union in 1951 and 2-year-long negotiations

Damages by the war, and atrocities committed against civilians

  • large number of casualties and deaths of South Korean, North Korean, UN, and Chinese soldiers
  • destruction of many mountains, fields, and cities
  • death and injuries of 2-3 millions of civilians
  • massacres of civilians under both North and South Korean occupation
  • destruction and ruin of the economy over the whole Korean Peninsula

The fire ceased, however...

  • signature of the ceasefire treaty on July 27, 1953 by the United States, North Korea, and China
  • failure to achieve a peace treaty between the political leaders of the involved countries
  • lack of agreement between the North and South at the Geneva Meeting in 1954
  • no further discussion on unification after 1954
